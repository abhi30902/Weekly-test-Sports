1. Bat: A cricket bat is used by the batsman to hit the ball. It is usually made of willow wood and comes in various shapes and sizes.

2. Ball: A cricket ball is made of leather and is hard. There are two types of cricket balls: red for test matches and white for limited-overs formats.

3. Stumps: Stumps are three wooden poles that are stuck into the ground. Bails are placed on top of them. Stumps are used to define the wicket.

4. Bails: Bails are small wooden pieces that are placed on top of the stumps. They must be dislodged by the ball for a batsman to be considered out.
